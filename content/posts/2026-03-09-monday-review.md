After yesterday's review session, today was dedicated to stepping back from active backend development and getting the rest of the project organized. 

Backend testing wrapped up successfully, which gave me a clean stopping point. With that foundation solid, I made the call to pause backend work temporarily and direct my full attention to the portfolio and project structure, both of which had been falling behind.

The bigger realization today was that working solo doesn't mean working without structure. I'd been logging decisions and issues throughout the project, but inconsistently and without a system that actually scaled. Now as we are mid project it became apparent that my portfolio was lacking at our monday review, which incidentally works out since this weeks highlight is our portfolio. I set up a GitHub Projects Kanban board to map out all the moving parts, something I'd used effectively on my last team project but hadn't prioritized here. The difference was immediate.

From here on out the the Friday reflection will be published as blog entires on the day to keep myself accountable and more consistent.

**This week's focus:** portfolio. Everything else waits.
