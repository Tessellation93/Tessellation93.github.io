This week delivered what I set out to do. The portfolio is updated, the Kanban board is populated and I have a clearer picture of where the project actually stands versus where I thought it stood.

The most useful thing about forcing myself to write up the last few weeks of work, wasn't the writing, it was the audit. Going back through my notes and turning them into readable entries made it obvious which decisions I'd made deliberately and which ones I'd made by default. There's a difference and it matters going forward. I feel more confident in my choices and although it is a larger workload than we've been used to, it feels inspiring to build my own project and nurture the more creative aspect of programming. The bricks are finally falling into place and there is a deeper understanding and given me the tools to fine tune my own process. I now appreciate and understand my teachers nudge at the monday review, writing alongside coding and refelcting actively on my progress was more important than I had deamed it. It is a tool and not yet another to do. Spuratically writting in notes and having a hard time finde a random Word document is a waste of time and it was holding me back.

A few things I resolved this week beyond the portfolio. I cleaned up the GitHub repo structure, wrote proper commit messages for the work that had been pushed without context and drafted the README to include a much more in depth description to give a proper introduction to my project. Small things, but they compound.

JavaJolt is in a better position now than it was on Monday and finally my portfolio is catching up.
